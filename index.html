About

I'm still becoming

My name is Natasha Nunn. I'm a writer, gardener, and tarot reader living on an acreage outside of Stony Plain, Alberta, with my three kids and my husband. By day I'm an academic librarian. In the early mornings — before any of that starts — I write.

I'll be honest: I don't really like writing about myself. Every time I have to write a bio I have a moment of dread. Do I really have to sell myself? I guess so.

I'm the youngest of three sisters, and being the baby of that trio definitely shaped me. Sometimes I still feel unformed, just trying to get attention. That feeling has faded as my own children have grown up and I've truly moved into adulthood — which, for me, didn't happen until after 40.

I write for readers like me: people who keep making what others might call stupid mistakes, who are conducting their own healing experiments without a guaranteed outcome. I'm not interested in triumph narratives. I'm interested in the courage it takes to keep going when you're not fully healed and never will be.

The Book

Seven nights.
Forty-five years.

Seven Parties is a memoir structured around seven pivotal parties spanning 1980 to 2013 — each one a waypoint in a life that refused to resolve into something tidy.

Alternating between those party chapters and present-day interludes — including the crisis involving her son's addiction and homelessness — the book examines how the same forces that wound us can also, somehow, heal us.

"For everyone who keeps making mistakes."

This is not a healing narrative. It's a book about the courage to continue living without being fully healed — about beautiful delusions, repeating cycles, and choosing, again and again, to stay in the experiment.
